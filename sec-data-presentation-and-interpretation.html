    Section 5.1 Data Presentation and Interpretation

    \(\textbf{Data representation}\) - is the process of arranging and presenting data in a way that makes it simple to comprehend and analyze. Diagrams, tables, charts, graphs and other visual formats can be used for this. Patterns, trends, and relationships in the data are easier to spot when they are properly represented.
    \(\textbf{Data Interpretation}\) - is the method for evaluating the represented data in order to derive significant findings. It involves understanding what the data indicates, spotting patterns and drawing well-informed conclusions from the results.

    Subsection 5.1.1 Drawing Bar Graphs of Data

    Activity 5.1.1.

    \({\color{black} \textbf{Work in groups}}\)
    1. Ask and record the number of members of your class, born in the months of January, February, March, April, May, and June.
    2. Fill in the table shown.
      Month Jan Feb Mar Apr May Jun
      No. of members
    3. Choose a suitable scale and represent the data on a bar graph.
    4. Compare and discuss your graphs with other groups.
    \({\color{green} \textbf{Key Takeaway}}\)
    A bar graph is the display of information using rectangles of uniform width. The length of each rectangle represents the frequency.
    Bars can be displayed either vertically or horizontally and are used to compare different categories or groups.

    Example 5.1.1.

    The following table shows the number of learners who received different grades in an exam:
    Grade Number of Learners
    5 45
    6 30

    8 30
    9 40
    Choose a suitable scale and represent the data on a bar graph.
    Solution.
    Choosing a suitable scale for the length of bars, for example 1cm representing 10 learner, the graph is drawn as shown.

    Subsection 5.1.2 Interpreting Bar Graphs of Data

    Activity 5.1.3.

    \({\color{black} \textbf{Work in groups}}\)
    A teacher surveyed a class of students about their favorite colors. The results are shown in the bar graph below.
    1. Which color was chosen by the highest number of learners?
    2. How many learners selected Green as their preferred color?
    3. What is the difference in the number of learners who chose Blue and those who chose Yellow?
    4. What is the total number of learners represented in the graph?
    5. What percentage of the total learners preferred the color Red?
    \({\color{black} \textbf{Key Takeaway}}\)
    The scale that is chosen, the width of the bars, and the length of the bars all provide information that is represented by a bar graph.

    Example 5.1.2.

    Mama Karisa went to Shinyalu market to buy fruits and placed them in a basket. The fruits were: Apples, Bananas, Oranges, Grapes, Mangoes, Pineapples, Passion and Avocado. A bar graph representing the information is given below. Use the information from the bar graph to answer the questions.
    1. Which fruit appears the most in the basket?
    2. How many oranges are in the basket?
    3. What is the difference between the number of apples and mangoes?
    4. What is the total number of fruits in the basket?
    5. Are there any fruits with the same quantity?
    6. How many more bananas would be needed to match the number of apples?
    7. If 10 apples and 5 oranges were removed, how many fruits would be left?
    8. How many more bananas and oranges are there than grapes and avocados?
    Solution.
    1. Compare all the given numbers in the fruits and see which is highest
      Apples = \(60\) are the most common fruit.
    2. Check the number of oranges in the graph
      there are \(56\) oranges
    3. Apples = \(60\)
      Mangoes = \(42\)
      Find the difference = \(60 - 42 = 18\)
      There are \(18\) more apples than mangoes.
    4. Add all the quantities together:
      \(60 + 45 + 56 + 38 + 42 + 50 + 35 + 40 = 366\)
      The total number of fruits in the basket is \(366\text{.}\)
    5. Check for equal number of fruits
      answer is NO
    6. Apples = \(60\)
      Bananas = \(45\)
      Difference = \(60 - 45 = 15 \)
      \(15\) more bananas are needed to match the apples.
    7. Apples = \(60 \) so Apples left = \(60 - 10 = 50 \)
      Oranges = \(56 \) so Oranges left = \(56 - 5 = 51 \)
      New total: \(50 + 45 + 51 + 38 + 42 + 50 + 35 + 40 = 351\)
      After removing some fruits, \(351\) fruits remain.
    8. Bananas + Oranges = \(45 + 56 = 101 \)
      Grapes + Avocados = \(38 + 40 = 78 \)
      Difference = \(101 - 78 = 23 \)
      There are \(23\) more bananas and oranges than grapes and avocados.

    Consider the graph below that shows the journey Wakavinye took from home to school
    1. How far from home was Wakavinye after 10 minutes?
    2. What was the total distance traveled after 30 minutes?
    3. How many kilometers did the Wakavinye travel between 15 and 20 minutes?
    4. What does the flat section of the graph represent?

    Example 5.1.4.

    A group of students conducted an experiment to observe how the temperature of water changes over time when heated. They recorded the temperature at different time intervals and plotted their results on a graph.
    1. What was the temperature at 0 minutes?
    2. How long did it take for the temperature to reach 0°C?
    3. During which time interval did the temperature stay constant?
    4. At what time did the temperature first reach 100°C?
    5. How many degrees did the temperature increase between 0 and 20 minutes?
    6. What does the flat section of the graph after 20 minutes indicate?
    7. If the trend continued beyond 25 minutes, what might happen to the temperature?
    Solution.
    1. From the graph,check the temperature at \(0\) minutes which was \(-25°C.\)
    2. The temperature started at \(-25°C\) and reached \(0°C\) at \(2\) minutes.
    3. From the graph, the temperature remained at \(0°C\) from \(2\) minutes to \(4\) minutes.
      The temperature also stayed at \(100°C\) from \(20\) minutes to \(25\) minutes.
    4. From the graph, the water temperature reached \(100°C\) at \(20\) minutes.
    5. At \(0\) minutes, the temperature was \(-25°C\text{.}\)
      At \(20\) minutes, the temperature was \(100°C\text{.}\)
      Increase in temperature = \(100°C - (-25°C) = 100°C + 25°C = 125°C \)
    6. A flat section on a temperature-time graph means the temperature is not changing.
      The flat section after \(20\) minutes indicates that the water has reached its boiling point and is turning into steam.
    7. If the heat source remains, the water will continue to boil.
      The temperature will stay at \(100°C\) until the water completely evaporates.

    Example 5.1.5.

    A bus and a car are traveling along the same route. Their distances from the starting point are recorded at different times in a 24-hour format and plotted on the graph. The graph shows when each vehicle was moving and when they took breaks.
    1. At what time did the bus start its journey?
    2. At what time did the car start its journey?
    3. What was the average speed of the car from 12:00 to 14:00?
    4. What was the speed of the bus between 13:00 and 17:00?
    5. How far had the bus traveled by 12:00?
    6. Between which times did the bus remain at the same distance?
    7. At what time did the car take a break?
    8. At what time did both the bus and the car reach 400 km?
    9. Which vehicle reached 500 km first?
    10. What was the overall average speed of the bus from 11:00 to 19:00?
    Solution.
    1. The bus started its journey at \(11:00\text{.}\)
    2. The car started its journey at \(12:00\text{.}\)
    3. The car traveled 100 km from \(12:00\) to \(14:00 (2 hours)\text{.}\)
      \(speed=\frac{100 km}{2 hours} = 50km/h\)
      The car traveled at an average speed of \(50 km/h\) from \(12:00\) to \(14:00\text{.}\)
    4. The bus traveled from \(100 km\) to \(400 km\) between \(13:00\) and \(17:00\text{.}\)
      Distance covered = \(400 km - 100 km = 300 km.\)
      Time taken = \(17:00 - 13:00 = 4 hours.\)
      \(speed=\frac{300 km}{4 hours} = 75km/h\)
      The bus traveled at an average speed of \(75 km/h\) between \(13:00\) and \(17:00\text{.}\)
    5. By \(12:00\text{,}\) the bus had traveled \(100 km\text{.}\)
    6. The bus remained stationary between \(12:00\) and \(13:00\text{,}\) staying at \(100 km\text{.}\)
    7. The car took a break between \(14:00\) and \(15:00\text{,}\) staying at \(100 km\text{.}\)
    8. Both the bus and the car reached \(400 km\) at \(17:00\text{.}\)
    9. The car reached \(500 km\) first at \(18:00\text{,}\) while the bus reached \(500 km\) at \(19:00\text{.}\)
    10. The bus covered \(500 km\) in \(8 hours\text{.}\)
      \(Average Speed=\frac{500 km}{8 hours} = 62.5km/h\)
      The overall average speed of the bus was \(62.5 km/h\text{.}\)

    Subsection 5.1.5 Identifying the Mode of a Set of Discrete Data

    Activity 5.1.6.

    \({\color{black} \textbf{Work in groups}}\)
    A mathematics teacher at Mukumu secondary school gave a short quiz to a class of 40 students, and these are their scores
    \(5, 7, 8, 10, 7, 6, 7, 9, 8, 7, 6, 9, 10, 7, 8, 9, 7, 6, 8\)
    \(9, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, 7, 6, 8, 9, 7, 6, 8, 9, 7, 6, 8, 9, 10, 7, 6\)

    2. Find the most frequent one
    3. Compare your answer with others in class
    \({\color{black} \textbf{Key Takeaway}}\)

    \(38, 40, 42, 39, 41, 40, 38, 42, 43, 40, 41, 39, 42, 40\)

    Find the mode of the data set.
    Solution.
    Shoe Size Tally Frequency
    38 |||| 4
    39 ||||| 5
    40 ||||| || 7
    41 ||||| | 6
    The mode of the data is \(7\)

    Subsection 5.1.6 Calculating the Mean of a Set of Discrete Data

    Activity 5.1.7.

    \({\color{black} \textbf{Work in groups}}\)
    A teacher recorded the ages of 45 students in a school. The ages are:
    \(12, 13, 12, 14, 13, 12, 15, 14, 13, 12, 14, 13, 12, 13, 14,\)
    \(12, 14, 12, 13, 14, 13, 12, 14, 13, 15, 12, 13, 15, 14,12,\)
    \(13, 12, 14, 13, 12, 15, 13, 14, 12, 13, 14, 12, 15, 14, 13\)
    1. Find the mean age of the students.
    2. Explain what the mean represents in this case.
    \({\color{black} \textbf{Key Takeaway}}\)
    The mean also known as average of a data set is the sum of all values divided by the total number of values.
    Formula for Mean is given by:
    \(\textbf{Mean} = \frac{\textbf{sum of all values}}{\textbf{Total number of values}}\)

    Example 5.1.7.

    A farmer recorded the number of oranges picked from \(45\) trees in his orchard. The number of apples picked from each tree is:
    \(50, 55, 60, 52, 48, 54, 58, 60, 55, 57, 53, 50, 52, 56, 55\)
    \(54, 55, 58, 60, 57, 54, 53, 55, 58, 60, 55, 52, 50, 53, 56\)
    \(60, 52, 50, 55, 57, 59, 54, 53, 56, 50, 55, 58, 52, 57, 54\)
    Find the mean number of oranges picked per tree.
    Solution.
    Add all the apple counts
    \(50+55+60+52+48+54+58+60+\)
    \(55+57+53+50+52+56+54+\)
    \(55+58+60+52+50+55+57\)
    \(+59+54+53+56+50+55+58+52+57\)
    \(+54+60+55+52+50+53+56\)
    \(+55+58+60+57+54+53+55\)
    \(=2467\)
    Divide by the total number of trees \(45\)
    \(\textbf{Mean} = \frac{2467}{45} = 54.82\)
    The mean number of apples picked per tree is \(54.82\text{.}\)

    Subsection 5.1.7 Determining the Median of a Set of Discrete Data

    Activity 5.1.8.

    \({\color{black} \textbf{Work in groups}}\)
    Taita secondary school football team coach recorded the heights (in cm) of \(25\) players on the team:
    \(175, 178, 180, 182, 185, 187, 190, 192, 195, 197, 200, 202,\)
    \(205, 207, 210, 212, 215, 217, 220, 222, 225, 227, 230, 232, 235\)
    1. Find the median height of the players.
    2. Explain what the median represents in this scenario
    \({\color{black} \textbf{Key Takeaway}}\)
    The item in the middle is the median when the items in a data set are sorted in ascending order.

    Example 5.1.8.

    A bakery recorded the number of loaves of bread sold each day for \(30\) days. The numbers are:
    \(50, 55, 60, 52, 58, 65, 70, 68, 72, 75, 80, 85, 90, 95, 100,\)
    \(105, 110, 98, 92, 88, 78, 66, 62, 54, 59, 74, 82, 87, 97, 102\)
    Find the median number of loaves sold per day.
    Solution.
    Arrange the data in ascending order
    \(50, 52, 54, 55, 58, 59, 60, 62, 65, 66, 68, 70, 72, 74, 75, \)
    \(78, 80, 82, 85, 87, 88, 90, 92, 95, 97, 98, 100, 102, 105, 110\)
    Find the middle values
    • Since there are \(30\) values (even number), the median is the average of the \(\text{15th}\) and \(\text{16th}\) values.
    • \(\text{15th}\) value = \(75\)
    • \(\text{16th}\) value = \(78\)
    \(\text{Median} = \frac{75 + 78}{2} = 76.5\)
    The median number of loaves sold per day is \(76.5\text{.}\)
