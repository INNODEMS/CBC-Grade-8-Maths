    Section 4.1 Geometrical Constructions

    Subsection 4.1.1 Definition

    Geometrical constructions are a way of creating geometric figures using only a compass and a straightedge. These constructions are based on a set of rules that allow you to create figures such as lines, angles, and polygons with a high degree of accuracy.

    Subsection 4.1.5 Angle Properties of Polgons

    In Grade 7 you learnt about properties of angles in a polygon; number of sides of polygons, interior angles of the polygons and the relationship between sides of polygons and interior angles.
    Here is a summary of what you learned from Grade 7
    In order to find Number of right angles and the sum of interior angles of any polygon:
    1. Identify the number of sides (\(n\)) of the regular polygon.
    2. Work out how many triangles could be created within the polygon by drawing lines from a single vertex to all the other vertices
    3. Multiply the number of triangles in the polygon by \(180^\circ\) to find the sum of the interior angles of the polygon.

    Example 4.1.4.

    Find the value of uknown angles marked in letters in the figure below.
    Solution.
    \(50^\circ, 60^\circ\) and \(y\) are exterior angles. There sum of interior angle and its corresponding exterior angle is \(180^\circ.\)
    Therefore:
    \begin{equation*} 50^\circ + x = 180^\circ \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} x = 180^\circ - 50^\circ \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} x = 130^\circ \end{equation*}
    The corresponding interior angle for angle \(60^\circ\) is:
    \begin{equation*} 180^\circ - 60^\circ = 120^\circ \end{equation*}
    To find the corresponding interior angle for \(y,\) in the pentagon, you know that interior angles in a pentagon add up to \(540^\circ.\)
    Therefore:
    \begin{equation*} 90^\circ + 90^\circ + 120^\circ + 130^\circ + (\text{ corresponding interior angle for }y) = 540^\circ. \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} 430^\circ + \text{ corresponding interior angle for }y = 540^\circ \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \text{ corresponding interior angle for } y = 540^\circ - 430^\circ \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \text{ corresponding interior angle for } y = 110^\circ \end{equation*}
    Since we have found the corresponding interior angle for \(y,\) to find the exterior angle \(y,\) we take;
    \begin{equation*} y = 180^\circ - \text{ corresponding interior angle for } y \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} y = 180^\circ - 110^\circ \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} y = 70^\circ \end{equation*}

    Example 4.1.5.

    Find the number of sides(n) of a regular polygon when all the interior angles of the polygon are \(135^\circ\)
    Solution.
    Method 1
    We can find the number if sides of a polygon from the given formula:
    \begin{equation*} \text{Exterior angle of the polygon }= \frac{360^\circ}{n} \end{equation*}
    First find the exterior angle of the polygon as shown:
    \begin{equation*} \text{Exterior angle of the polygon } = 180^\circ - \text{interior angle of the polygon} \end{equation*}
    Therefore:
    \begin{equation*} \text{Exterior angle of the polygon } = 180^\circ - 140^\circ \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \text{Exterior angle of the polygon } = 40^\circ \end{equation*}
    Thus, using this formula:
    \begin{equation*} \text{Exterior angle of the polygon }= \frac{360^\circ}{n} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} 40^\circ = \frac{360^\circ}{n} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} n = \frac{360^\circ}{40^\circ} \end{equation*}
    Therefore; \(n = 9 \)
    Method 2
    Alternatively, we can find the number of sides of the polygon as shown below:
    \begin{equation*} \text{ Sum of interior angles = Size of interior angle } \times \text{n} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \text{ Sum of interior angles } = 140^\circ \times{n} = 140n \end{equation*}
    Also, sum of interior angles can be given by multiplying \(180^\circ\) by the number of trialgles \((n - 2)\) in the polygon.
    \begin{equation*} 180^\circ(n-2) \end{equation*}
    Therefore:
    \begin{equation*} 180^\circ(n-2) = 140n \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} 180n - 360 = 140n \end{equation*}
    Collecting like terms, we get:
    \begin{equation*} 180n - 140n = 360 \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} 40n = 360 \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} n = \frac{360}{40} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} n = 9 \end{equation*}

    Subsection 4.1.7 Construction of Irregular Polygons

    An irregular polygon does not have equal sides and equal angles.

    Subsubsection 4.1.7.1 Construction of Irregular Triangles

    There are different types of irregular triangles namely: isosceles, scalene and right triangle.
    Properties of isoscles triangle.
    1. Only two sides are equal in length.
    2. Opposite angles of the triangle are equal.
    3. The sum of the angles add up to \(180^\circ.\)
    Properties of scalene triangle.
    1. All the three sides have different length.
    2. All the angles have different sizes.
    3. The sum of the angles add up to \(180^\circ.\)
    Properties of right triangle.
    1. The sides are not equal in length.
    2. One of the angle measures \(90^\circ.\)
    3. The sum of the angles add up to \(180^\circ\)
