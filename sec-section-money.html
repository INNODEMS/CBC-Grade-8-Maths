    Interest is Ksh. \(10\, 000\text{.}\)

    Subsection 3.3.2 simple intrest(1)

    In the previous section, you identified types of interest and terms related to intrest such as the \(\textbf{principal,the amount,the rate }\) and \(\textbf{the time}\text{.}\)

    Subsubsection 3.3.2.1 Calculating simple interest in real life situations

    Activity 3.3.2.
    \(\textbf{ Work in groups}\)
    What you need: Writing materials.
    Ekadeli deposited \(sh \, 20\,000 \) a bank account for one year. The bank paid interset ate a rate of \(7\%\) of the deposit per annum.
    1. Identify the principal, the rate and the time from the information
    2. \(\textbf{Note:}\) Rate of \(10\% \) per annum means for evry \(sh\, 100\) principal, you earn an interest of sh \(sh \, 10 \) in one year.
      1. How much interest will \(sh \,100\) earn in one year at a rate of \(7\%\text{?}\)
      2. How many hundreds are in principa of \(sh\, 20\,000\text{?}\)
      3. How much interest will \(sh\, 20 \,000\) earn in one year?
    3. Suppose Ekadeli had deposited the same \(sh\, 20\,000\) for two years at the same rate of \(7\%\) per annum. How much interest would the money earn?
      1. Identify a formula that you can use to calculate the interest.
      2. What do we call this type of interest?
    5. Discuss and share your results withother groups.
    \({\color{black} \textbf{Key Takeaway:}}\)
    \(\textbf{Simple intrest(I)}\) is the interest calculated only on the initial amount of money, called the principal, over a period of time at a fixed rate.
    The principal \(P\) is the original amount of money invested or borrowed.
    The rate (\(R\)) is the percentage of the principal charged as interest per year. It is usually expressed as a percentage per annum (\(\textbf{P.A}\)).
    The time (\(T\)) is the duration for which the money is invested or borrowed, typically measured in years.
    The formula for calculating simple interest is:
    \begin{align*} \text{Simple intrest }(I) =\amp \text{Principal} \times \frac{\text{Rate}}{100} \times \text{Time} \\ I= \amp \frac{PRT}{100} \end{align*}
    Example 3.3.3.
    Franny invests Ksh \(16\,000\) in a savings account. She earns a simple interest rate of \(14\%,\) paid annually on her investment. She intends to hold the investment for \(1\frac{1}{2}\)years. How much simple interest did the invest earn?.
    Solution.
    The principal invested(P) \(= \text{Ksh}. \, 16\,000 \)
    Rate(R) \(= 14\%\)
    Time(T) \(=1\frac{1}{2}\)
    \begin{align*} \text{Simple interest}= \amp \frac{P \times R \times T }{100} \\ =\amp 16\,000 \times \frac{14}{100} \times 1\frac{1}{2} \\ =\amp 16\,000 \times \frac{14}{100} \times \frac{3}{2} \\ =\amp \text{Ksh.}\, 3\,360 \end{align*}
    Simple interest earned \(= {\color{blue} \text{Ksh.}\, 3\,360}\)
    Example 3.3.4.
    Isadora is a maize farmer. She harvested several bags of maize and sold some of them for \(sh.\, 850\,000\text{.}\) She deposited the money in her account in a certain bank for nine months. The bank paid simple interest at a rate of \(6\%\) per annum.
    1. How much intersest did the money earn in the nine months?
    2. What was the amount in her account at the end of the nine months?
    Solution.
    1. Principal(P) \(= \text{Ksh} 850\,000\)
      Rate(R) \(= 6\%\)
      Time(T) \(= 9 \text{months}\text{.}\) Express the months as a fraction of the year because the rate is per annum.
      So, time \(=\frac{9}{12}= \frac{3}{4}\) years.
      \begin{align*} \text{Simple interest}= \amp \frac{P \times R \times T }{100} \amp \\ =\amp 850\,000 \times \frac{6}{100} \times \frac{3}{4}\\ =\amp sh. 38\,250 \end{align*}
      The interest earned is \({\color{blue} sh. 38\,250}\)
    2. Amount is gotten by sum of \(\text{principal} + \text{interest}\text{.}\)
      \begin{align*} \text{Amount}=\amp \text{Principal} + \text{Interest} \\ =\amp sh\, 850\,000+ sh\, 38\,250\\ = \amp sh\, 888\,250 \end{align*}
      The total amount in Isdora’s account is \({\color{blue} sh\, 888\,250 }\text{.}\)

    \({\color{black} \textbf{Key Takeaway:}}\)
    \(\text{Simple interest}= \text{principal} \times \text{rate} \times \text{time} \text{...}\)(i)
    1. Divide both sides of the equation...(i) by \(\textbf{rate} \times \textbf{time }\) to get:
      \begin{equation*} \text{Principal}= \frac{\text{Interest}}{ \text{rate} \times \text{time}}\text{.} \end{equation*}
    2. Divide both sides of the equation...(i) by \(\textbf{principal} \times \textbf{time }\) to get:
      \begin{equation*} \text{Rate}= \frac{\text{Interest}}{ \text{principal} \times \text{time}}\text{.} \end{equation*}
    3. Divide both sides of the equation...(i) by \(\textbf{principal} \times \textbf{rate }\) to get:
      \begin{equation*} \text{Time}= \frac{\text{Interest}}{ \text{principal} \times \text{rate}}\text{.} \end{equation*}

    Example 3.3.5.

    Murunga deposited money in a bank for two years. The bank paid a simple interest at a rate of \(10\%\) per annum. He earned simple interest of \(sh \, 24\,000\text{.}\) What was the pricipal amount?
    Solution.
    Let the amount deposited be P
    \(I= sh\,24 \,0000\)
    \(R= 10\%\)
    \(T=2 \,\text{years}\)
    \begin{align*} \text{Principal(P)}= \amp \frac{\text{Interest}}{\text{rate} \times \text{time}} \\ =\amp \frac{sh\, 24\,000}{10\% \times 2} \qquad \textbf{-------}>\textbf{Divide both the numerator and the denominator by 2.}\\ =\amp \frac{sh\, 12\,000}{\frac{10}{100}} \qquad \textbf{-------}> 10\% = \frac{\textbf{10}}{\textbf{100}} \\ = \amp sh\, 12\,000 \times \frac{100}{10} \\ = \amp \textbf{sh} \, 120 \,000 \end{align*}
    The principal amount was \({\color{blue} \textbf{sh} \, 120 \,000}\)

    Example 3.3.6.

    Talu sold her car for \(sh\, 1\,000\,000\text{.}\) She deposited the money in a bank. She earned a simple interest of \(sh\, 240 \, 000\) after three years. Calculate the rate at which the bank paid the simple interest.
    Solution.

    \(P= sh\,1\,000 \,000\)
    \(I= sh\,240\,0000\)
    \(T= 3 \,\text{years}\)
    \(R=\text{?}\)
    \begin{align*} \text{Rate}(R)= \amp \frac{\text{Interest}}{\text{principal} \times \text{time}} \\ =\amp \frac{sh\, 240\,000}{sh\,1\,000\,000 \times 3}\\ =\amp \frac{8}{100} \qquad \textbf{-------} > \textbf{Because R is expressed as a percentage, convert the fraction} \frac{8}{100}\,\textbf{to percentage by multiplying by 100%}\\ = \amp 8\% \end{align*}
    The simple interest rate is \({\color{blue} 8\% }\text{.}\)

    Example 3.3.7.

    Munyao deposited \(sh\, 25\,000\) in a bank that paid simple interest at rate of \(9\%\) per annum. The deposit earned \(sh\, 6\,750 \) as simple interest. For how long did he deposit the money?

    \(I= sh\,6\,750\)

    The duration it take is \({\color{blue} \text{3 years}}\)

    Subsection 3.3.4 Compound interest

    In the previous sections, you learnt how to calculate simple interest.
    In this section, you will learn how to calculate another type of interest called \(\textbf{compound interest.}\)

    Subsubsection 3.3.4.1 Calculating compound interest step by step per annum in real life situation

    Activity 3.3.4.
    \(\textbf{work in groups}\)
    Consider a trader who deposited \(Ksh. \, 50 \,000\) in a bank in a certain year.
    1. If interest of \(5\%\) was earned that year, calculate the interest.
    2. Find the total amount of money in the account the end of that year.
    3. In the second year, the trader earned \(5\%\) interest on the total amount i the first year.
    4. In the third year, the trader earned \(5\%\) interest on the total amount in the second year.
    5. Find the total amount of money in the account at the end of the third year.
    6. Subtract the initial deposit from the total amount of money in the account at the end of the third year.
    7. Discuss and compare result with other groups.
    \({\color{black} \textbf{Key Takeaway:}}\)
    \(\textbf{Compound interest}\) is the interest where the principal keeps changing each year.
    The principal of any year is the sum of the principal and the interest of the previous year.
    \(\textbf{Compound interest}\) is the total interest earned in all the year.
    The difference between the amount derived from accumulated interest and the initial amount is the compound interest.
    \(\textbf{N/B:}\) \(\textbf{Simple Interest}\) is calculated only on the original principal, while \(\textbf{Compound Interest}\) is calculated on the principal plus accumulated interest, making it grow over time that the principal was deposited.
    Example 3.3.8.
    A trader took a loan of \(Ksh. \, 400\,000\) at the rate of \(12\%\) compound interest for three year. Find the total amount of interest repaid after three years.
    Solution.
    \begin{align*} \text{Interest at the end of the first year}=\amp \frac{P_1 RT}{100} \\ =\amp 400\,000 \times \frac{12}{100} \times 1 \\ =\amp Ksh\, 48\,000 \end{align*}
    \begin{align*} \textbf{Principle}(P_2)=\amp \textbf{Initial principal}(P_1)+ \textbf{interest} \\ = \amp Ksh. \, 400\,000 + Ksh\, 48 \, 000 \\ =\amp Ksh. \, 448\,000 \end{align*}
    \begin{align*} \text{Interest at the end of the second year}=\amp \frac{P_2 RT}{100} \\ =\amp Ksh. \, 448\,000 \times \frac{12}{100} \times 1\\ =\amp Ksh.\, 53\,760 \end{align*}
    \begin{align*} \textbf{Principle}(P_3)=\amp \textbf{Initial principal}(P_2)+ \textbf{interest} \\ =\amp Ksh. \, 448\,000 + Ksh. \, 53\,760 \\ =\amp Ksh. \, 501\,760 \end{align*}
    \begin{align*} \text{Interest at the end of the third year}(P_3)=\amp \frac{P_3 RT}{100} \\ =\amp Ksh. 501\,760 \times \frac{12}{100} \times 1 \\ =\amp Ksh. 60\,211.20 \end{align*}
    Therefore,
    \begin{align*} \textbf{Compound interest}= \amp Ksh.(48\,000+53\, 760+60\,211.20)\\ = \amp {\color{blue} Ksh. 161\,971.20 } \end{align*}
    Example 3.3.9.
    Akinyi deposited \(sh \,60 \,000\) in a bank. The bank paid interest at the rate of \(10\%\) per annum. He withdrew all the money at the end of three years.
    1. Calculate the compound interest after the three years.
    2. How much money was in his account by the end of the three years?
    Solution.
    1. \(\text{Simple interest} = \text{principal} \times \text{rate} \times \text{time}\)
      \begin{align*} \text{First year's interest} =\amp sh\, 60\,000 \times \frac{10}{100} \times 1 \\ =\amp sh\, 6\,000 \end{align*}
      \begin{align*} \text{Second year's interest} =\amp (sh\, 60\,000 + sh\, 6\,000 )\times \frac{10}{100} \times 1 \\ = \amp sh\, 66\,000 \times \frac{10}{100} \times 1\\ = \amp sh\,6\,600 \end{align*}
      \begin{align*} \text{Third year's interest} =\amp (sh\, 66\,000 + sh\, 6\,600 )\times \frac{10}{100} \times 1 \\ = \amp sh\,72\,600 \times \frac{10}{100} \times 1 \\ = \amp sh\, 7\,260 \end{align*}
      \begin{align*} \text{Compound interest}= \amp \text{total interest earned in the three years.} \\ =\amp sh\,6\,000+sh\,6\,600+sh\,7\,260 \\ =\amp {\color{blue} sh\, 19\,860 } \end{align*}
    2. \begin{align*} \textbf{Amount after three years}=\amp \textbf{initial principal} + \textbf{total interest} \\ = \amp sh\,60\,0000+sh\, 19\,860 \\ = \amp {\color{blue} sh\,79\,860} \end{align*}
