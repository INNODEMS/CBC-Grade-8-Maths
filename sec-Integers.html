    Section 1.1 Integers

    Integers are a fundamental concept in mathematics with wide applications in real-life situations. In sports and games, points, scores, and rankings are often represented using integers. In Grade \(7\text{,}\) you learned about whole numbers. In this section, we will take a step further to explore integers.

    Objectives

    By the end of this substrand, you should be able to:
    1. Represent integers on a number line.
    2. Use a number line to perform addition and subtraction of integers.
    3. Solve expressions involving combined operations of integers using a number line.

    Subsection 1.1.1 Identifying Integers in Different Situations

    Activity 1.1.1.

    Work in groups
    1. Choose a starting point on a level ground.
    2. From the starting point, take three steps to the right, marking each step.
    3. Return to the starting point and take six steps forward to the right, marking each step.
    4. From the starting point take four steps to the left, marking each step.
    5. Consider the starting point as \(0\) , assign a positive sign \((+)\) to steps taken to the right direction and assign a negative sign \((-)\) to the steps taken to the left direction.
    6. Compare your results with other groups results and discuss what you observed.

    Definition 1.1.1.

    Integers are set of positive numbers, negatives numbers and zero.
    \(\textbf{ Key Takeaway }\)
    • All whole numbers are integers, but not all integers are whole numbers because integers also include negative numbers.
    • Integers do not include fractions or decimals.

    Subsection 1.1.3 Addition of integers on a Number Line

    Activity 1.1.3.

    Work in groups
    1. Draw a number line similar to the one shown below.
    2. Starting at \((+1)\) on the number line, move \(3\) steps to the right and circle the number at the end of the \(3rd\) step.
    3. Starting at \((-5)\) on the number line, move \(7\) steps to the right and circle the number at the end of the \(7th\) step.
    4. What operation do you notice in step \(2\) and in step \(3\text{?}\)
    5. Discuss and share your findings with the rest of the class.
    \(\textbf{Key Takeaway}\)
    • Consider \((+2) + (+3)\text{:}\) when adding \((+3)\text{,}\) a positive integer, to \((+2)\text{,}\) we start at \((+2)\) on the number line and move \(3\) steps to the right.
    • Consider \((+1) + (-5)\text{:}\) when adding \((-5)\text{,}\) a negative integer, to \((+1)\text{,}\) we start at \((+1)\) on the number line and move \(5\) steps to the left.

    Example 1.1.4.

    Use a number line to work out the following:
    1. \(\displaystyle (-8) + (+3)\)
    2. \(\displaystyle (+1) + (-4)\)
    Solution.
    1. \((-8) + (+3)\)
      Draw the number line as shown below.
      Locate the \((-8)\) on the number line.
      From the \((-8)\text{,}\) move \(3\) spteps to the right, because the second number \((+3)\) is positive.
      \begin{equation*} (-8) + (+3) = (-5) \end{equation*}
    2. \((+1) + (-4)\)
      Draw the number line as shown below.
      Locate the \((+1)\) on the number line.
      From the \((+1)\text{,}\) move \(4\) spteps to the right, because the second number \((-4)\) is negative.
      \begin{equation*} (+1) + (-4) = (-3) \end{equation*}

    Subsection 1.1.4 Subtraction of Integers on a Number Line

    Activity 1.1.4.

    Work in groups
    1. Draw a number line similar to the one shown below.
    2. Starting at \((+5)\) on the number line, move \(3\) steps to the left and circle the number at the end of the \(3rd\) step.
    3. Starting at \((+1)\) on the number line, move \(4\) steps to the left and circle the number at the end of the \(4th\) step.
    4. What operation do you notice in step \(2\) and in step \(3\text{?}\)
    5. Discuss and share your findings with the rest of the class.
    \(\textbf{Key Takeaway}\)
    • Consider \((-3) - (+7)\text{:}\) when subtracting \((+7)\text{,}\) a positive integer, from \((-3)\text{,}\) we begin at \((-3)\) on the number line and move \(7\) steps to the left.

    Example 1.1.5.

    Using a number line, work out the following:
    1. \(\displaystyle (+5) - (3)\)
    2. \(\displaystyle (-7) - (-5)\)
    Solution.
    1. \((+5) - (3)\)
      We start at \(+5\) as our initial point.
      Since we are subtracting \(+3\text{,}\) we move \(3\) steps to the left of the number line and stop at \(+2\)
      \begin{equation*} (+5) - (3) = (+2) \end{equation*}
    2. \((-7) - (-5)\)
      We start at \(-7\) as our initial point.
      Since we are subtracting \(-5\text{,}\) we move \(5\) steps to the right of the number line and stop at \(-2\text{.}\)
      \begin{equation*} (-7) - (-5) = (-2) \end{equation*}

    Subsection 1.1.5 Combined Operations of Integers on a Number Line

    Activity 1.1.5.

    Work in groups
    1. Draw a number line similar to the one shown below.
    2. Starting at \((-2)\) on the number line, move \(7\) steps to the right and then move \(3\) steps to left and circle the number at the end of the last step.
    3. Starting at \((+2)\) on the number line, move \(6\) steps to the right and then move \(2\) steps to left and circle the number at the end of the last step.
    4. Write down the expressions and the final answers of the movements in step \(2\) and step \(3\text{.}\)
    5. Share your results and compare them with other groups in the class.
    \(\textbf{Key Takeaway}\)
    When solving expressions that involve both addition and subtraction of integers, follow the operations from left to right.
      • Adding a positive integer means moving to the right.
      • Subtracting a positive integer means moving to the left.
      • Adding a negative integer is the same as moving to the left.
      • Subtracting a negative integer is the same as moving to the right.
    • Example: \((-2) + (+5) - (+3)\) Start at \(-2\text{,}\) move \(5\) steps to the right (for \(+5\)), then move \(3\) steps to the left again since we are subtracting a positive integer.

    Example 1.1.6.

    Use a number line to perform the following expression.
    \((+4) - (6) + (3) \)
    Solution.
    Starting at \((+4)\text{,}\) move \(6\) steps to the left since we are subtracting a positive integer as shown in the number line below.
    Then, from \((-2)\) on the number line, move \(3\) steps to the right since we are adding a a positive integer.
