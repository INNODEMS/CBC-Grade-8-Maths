    Section 3.1 Circles

    Subsection 3.1.1 circumference of a circle

    \({\color{blue}\text{Circumference}}\) is the total distance distance around ta circle.
    It is like the perimeter of a circle.

    Activity 3.1.1.

    Think of a hula hoop. The circumference is the length of the edge of a hula hoop if you could cut it and stretch it into a straight line.

    Exploration 3.1.2.

    Circumference = \(\pi d \text{ where,} \pi =\frac{22}{7} or 3.14\) or \(2 \pi r\text{.}\) \(d\) is the diameter and \(r\) is the radius.
    Find the circumference of the following circle given the radius \(r = 56 \text{ cm }\text{.}\) Use \(\pi = \frac {22}{7}.\)
    \begin{align*} \text {Circumference } = \amp \pi d + d\\ = \amp \frac {22}{7} \times 112 \text{ cm } \\ = \amp \frac {2464}{7} \text{ cm } \\ = \amp 352 \text{ cm } \end{align*}
    OR
    \begin{align*} \text {Circumference } = \amp 2 \pi \text { r } \\ \amp \\ = \amp \frac {22}{7} \times 56 \text{ cm } \\ = \amp \frac {44 \times 56}{7} \text{ cm } \\ = \amp \frac {2464}{7} \text{ cm } + 112 \text { cm }\\ = \amp 352 \text{ cm } \end{align*}
    Find the circumference of the following circles. Use \(\pi = \frac {22}{7}.\)

    Example 3.1.1.

    The radius of a flower garden is \(6\)m. What length of barbed wire can fence the garden using five strands of the barbed wire? \((\text{use} \pi = 3.142)\)
    Solution.
    \begin{align*} \text{ Circumference is } 2 \pi r= \amp 2 \times \frac {22}{7} \times 3.142 \times 6 \text {m}\\ = \amp 37.704 \text{m} \\ \text { Total length of barbed wire } = \amp 37.704 \text{ m} \times 5\\ = \amp 188.52 \text{m} \end{align*}

    Subsection 3.1.2 Length of an arc of a circle 1

    In the previous section we have explored about getting perimeter that is the circumference of a circle. Can you remember how you did it?
    In this section we will calculate the arc length of two sectors namely,semi-circle and quarter circle.
    An arc is a part of the circumference of a circle. The \(\textbf {circumference} \) is the total distance around the circle, and an arc is just a section of that curved edge.
    example quarter

    Activity 3.1.3.

    • What you require: Circular cut out of a known diameter , a pair of scissors or razor blade, a ruler and a piece of string.
    Calculate the circumference of the cut out by measuring the crcular edge using a string.
    Use a ruler to measure the length of the string.
    Cut the cutout into two equal parts along the diameter. This enables you obtain two equal objects called a \(\textbf { Semi-circle.}\)
    Measure the curved length of one semi-circle using a string. Use a ruler to measure the length of the string.
    Measure the angle of the at the centre O of the semicircle. Express the angle as a fraction of \(360 ^\circ\text{.}\)
    Compare the answer you got in step 3 and the answer you got in step 4. What do you notice?
    Discuss how to calculate the length of the arc of the semi-circle.
    A semi-circle is a sector of a circle. Where the term sector is derived from the word section.
    Hint 1.
    Arc length of a semi-circle is given by \(\frac {180^ \circ} {360^ \circ} \times \pi \text { d or} \frac {180^ \circ} {360^ \circ} \times 2 \pi r = \frac {1}{2} \pi \text{ d or } \frac {1}{2}(2 \pi r)\)
    Do an extended activity to explore on how to calculate the length of the arc of a quarter circle by simply cutting the semi-circle and repeat the steps of measuring circumference length
    A quarter a circle is called a quadrant. So how many Quadrants do we have? \(\textbf{ 4 }.\)
    A quarter-circle is also a sector of a circle.
    Hint 2.
    Arc length of a quarter-circle is \(\frac {90^ \circ} {360^ \circ} \times \pi \text{ d or } \frac {90^ \circ} {360^ \circ} \times 2 \pi r = \frac {1}{4} \pi \text{ d or } \frac {1}{4}(2 \pi r)\)
    Can you compute one for a three quarter-circle?
